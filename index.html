Why Lofoten?

Mountains and arctic fjords

Lofoten is an archipelago and a traditional district in the county of Nordland, Norway. Lofoten has distinctive scenery with dramatic mountains and peaks, open sea and sheltered bays, beaches and untouched lands. There are two towns, Svolvær and Leknes - the latter is approximately 169km north of the Arctic Circle and approximately 2420km away from the North Pole. The archipelago experiences one of the world's largest elevated temperature anomalies relative to its high latitude.

Hiking

"Mannen" was one of my first Lofoten hikes. Here you get an absolutely incredible view of some of the finest Vestvågøy and perhaps Lofoten has to offer. The eye-catcher is primarily Haukland beach down to the south, but Uttakleivstranden on the north side of Mannen is also a great sight. Not to mention the endless horizon to the west and all the beautiful mountains around. The view would probably have presented itself even better with better weather, but we were still impressed and were glad that we had been able to experience this trip as well.
